The 1980s introduced self-bailing rafts, a massive technological leap that made navigating turbulent rapids much safer. This innovation allowed rafters to conquer extreme rivers, cementing rafting as a mainstream global extreme sport.

History

White-water rafting began in 1811 with a failed attempt to navigate Wyoming’s Snake River. By the 1940s, military-surplus inflatable rafts revolutionized the activity, turning a dangerous necessity into an exciting recreational sport.

Commercial rafting surged in the 1960s, gaining global popularity and making its Olympic debut in 1972. Today, it is a thriving worldwide adventure industry blending high-adrenaline thrills with pristine wilderness exploration.
